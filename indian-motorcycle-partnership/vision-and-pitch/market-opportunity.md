**1. Manufacturer Scan Tools**
- **Examples**: Indian/Polaris Digital Wrench, H-D Digital Technician, BMW Motorrad Diagnostic System
- **Pricing**: $3,000 - $8,000 per system
- **Pros**: Brand-specific, deep integration
- **Cons**: Expensive, single-brand only, no AI/AR
- **Market Share**: ~40% of dealer market

**2. Aftermarket Scan Tools**
- **Examples**: Texa, Autel, Snap-on motorcycle modules
- **Pricing**: $2,000 - $5,000 per system
- **Pros**: Multi-brand support, established brands
- **Cons**: Limited motorcycle focus, no AI, expensive updates
- **Market Share**: ~35% of market

**3. Mobile Apps and Software**
- **Examples**: Various OBD-II readers, basic code scanners
- **Pricing**: $50 - $500
- **Pros**: Inexpensive, mobile-friendly
- **Cons**: Limited functionality, not professional-grade
- **Market Share**: ~15% (growing in independent segment)

**4. Service Information Systems**
- **Examples**: AllData, Mitchell1, Identifix
- **Pricing**: $2,000 - $5,000 per year subscription
- **Pros**: Comprehensive repair information
- **Cons**: Primarily automotive, limited motorcycle content
- **Market Share**: ~10% of motorcycle shops
